Who I Am

I'm a senior Mechanical Engineering student who is also minoring in Computer Science, and I'm looking to continue my studies in graduate school as a Master's ME student. My interests include mechanical design, control systems, and mechatronics.
To the right, you will find my extracurriculars and hobbies.

I love exploring and learning new things. I think that's what makes me love engineering. One of my hobbies is hiking as it's a great destressor and always a fun way to explore new places! Seen above I am trying my best not to fall in the river, where the ice cold Icelandic waters await.
I am part of the only co-ed engineering fraternity on the UC Davis campus. They are my home away from home and have pushed me to greater heights both personally and professionally. I continue to give back through taking on several leadership roles that have taught me time management skills, teamwork, conflict resolution, and delegation.
One of my greatest passions that I have repurposed into my engineering journey is my love for reading and writing. Specifically, I enjoy reading fantasy and sci-fi novels, and I enjoy writing short stories and attempting novels. I think it takes a certain kind of imagination to dream up of an entirely new world with unique characters and back stories. This sort of creativity is something that I held dear in my childhood writing all sorts of stories and poems, and I believe it has grown and insired me in my engineering endeavors.
While many might not term this a "hobby" per se, I believe one of the things I love to do is try new things! I am always open to new experiences, and many times saying yes to something new that may be intimidating has turned out amazing! Some examples include deciding to study abroad in Iceland for a summer, going to various cultural and food fairs with friends, travelling to Oregon to watch the eclipse, and (seen above) attending a lights festival.
